  <h3>  Fourth Place </h3>
  <p>3D Spatial Analysis of Age-Adjusted Depression Rates (2021-2022)</p>
    <p>Map-Maker: Tanmay Kumar Sahu</p>
  <p> This 3D spatial visualization highlights the severe mental health disparities across New York City's 
    42 UHF districts. By comparing Age-Adjusted Depression Rates (2021-2022) alongside the Heat Vulnerability Index (HVI), 
    this dashboard exposes a critical vulnerability gap. The data reveals a predictable escalation: as a neighborhood's
    environmental heat risk increases, its baseline depression rate rises in tandem, pinpointing exactly where intersectional 
    intervention is needed most.</p>

 <h3>   Fifth Place </h3>
  <p> Changes in Depression Rates Across New York City: 2017–2018 vs 2021–2022</p>
    <p>Map-Maker: Raghed Soliman</p>
  <p> "Depression rates increased in multiple NYC boroughs between 2017–2018 and 2021–2022, with the strongest changes appearing in high density urban neighborhoods.”

This map compares adult depression rates across New York City using data from 2017–2018 and 2021–2022. It visualizes differences across boroughs and UHF42 neighborhoods to show how mental health patterns have changed over time.

Depression rates are displayed using a choropleth map, where darker colors represent higher percentages. The comparison highlights geographic disparities and shifts in mental health across NYC communities.

The goal of this map is to increase awareness of mental health inequality and provide a clear understanding of how depression varies across New York City and across different communities.
</p>
